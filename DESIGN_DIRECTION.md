# Beyond Japanese — Design & Brand Direction

## Core Brand Identity

Beyond Japanese is not a startup-style language platform.
It is a warm, personal, Kyoto-inspired Japanese learning experience.

The atmosphere should feel:

- warm
- calm
- human
- thoughtful
- emotionally soft
- culturally grounded
- approachable
- slightly handcrafted
- personal rather than corporate

The site should feel like:
“a quiet cultural experience”
—not a SaaS dashboard or luxury fashion brand.

---

# Emotional Direction

Users should feel:

- welcomed
- safe
- curious about Japan
- emotionally connected
- inspired to explore Japan more deeply
- guided by a real person

The site should NOT feel:

- overly polished
- sterile
- ultra minimal
- corporate
- startup-like
- luxury hotel branding
- AI generated
- aggressive
- trendy tech aesthetic

Avoid “premium luxury” aesthetics.
Avoid hard modernism.

Keep warmth and softness.

---

# Visual Tone

The current site already has the correct emotional direction.

Future edits should:
- refine
- organize
- improve hierarchy
- improve spacing
- improve readability

BUT should NEVER:
- redesign into a different aesthetic
- replace the emotional tone
- modernize excessively
- make it feel like a startup landing page

This is refinement, NOT rebranding.

---

# Typography Direction

## Overall

Typography should feel:
- elegant
- calm
- soft
- editorial
- human

Avoid:
- geometric startup fonts
- overly bold sans-serif
- ultra-modern typography
- aggressive tracking
- tight condensed headlines

---

## Hero Section Typography

The hero heading should feel:
- expressive
- emotional
- immersive
- slightly cinematic

But still:
- readable
- warm
- natural

Avoid:
- extremely heavy bold fonts
- tech-company typography
- futuristic typography

The current issue:
the text became too bold and too geometric.

Reduce:
- font weight
- visual heaviness
- corporate feeling

---

## Preferred Font Style

Use:
- elegant editorial serif for emotional accents
- soft modern sans-serif for body text

Good references:
- Cormorant Garamond
- Instrument Sans
- General Sans
- Neue Montreal
- Source Sans 3
- Inter (only lightly)

Avoid:
- Plus Jakarta Sans in heavy weights
- Poppins
- Montserrat
- overly geometric sans-serif

---

# Color Direction

The existing color palette should remain mostly unchanged.

Main emotional palette:
- warm ivory
- muted cream
- soft Kyoto green
- deep teal-blue
- warm brick red
- soft brown neutrals

Avoid:
- pure black
- harsh white
- neon colors
- saturated gradients
- startup blue

---

# Layout Direction

The site should breathe naturally.

Use:
- generous whitespace
- calm spacing rhythm
- editorial layout pacing
- asymmetry only when subtle

Avoid:
- overly rigid grids
- dense sections
- crowded layouts
- too many cards
- excessive borders

---

# Imagery Direction

Photography is one of the most important parts of the brand.

Images should feel:
- authentic
- lived-in
- warm
- atmospheric
- quiet
- observational

Preferred imagery:
- Kyoto streets
- cafes
- real conversations
- soft natural light
- trains
- shrines
- daily Japanese moments
- handwritten notes
- study scenes

Avoid:
- stock-photo corporate smiles
- luxury hotel imagery
- fake AI lifestyle photos
- overly cinematic dark grading
- ultra minimalist interiors

Photos should feel:
“someone genuinely exploring Japan.”

---

# Ayumi Section

Ayumi should feel:
- approachable
- warm
- intelligent
- trustworthy
- calm

NOT:
- luxury influencer
- corporate founder
- polished executive

Portrait treatment should feel:
- natural
- lightly editorial
- softly framed
- personal

Subtle handcrafted feeling is welcome.

---

# Motion & Interaction

Animations should be:
- subtle
- slow
- gentle
- calm

Good:
- fade in
- soft hover
- slight image movement
- soft transitions

Avoid:
- flashy animations
- parallax overload
- aggressive motion
- modern startup microinteractions

---

# CTA Direction

CTA buttons should feel:
- inviting
- warm
- human

NOT:
- aggressive
- sales-heavy
- conversion-hack style

The primary CTA is:
"Book Your Trial Lesson"

Secondary CTAs should be minimal.

---

# Important Rules

DO NOT:
- rewrite copy
- shorten emotional writing
- modernize the voice
- replace warmth with minimalism
- add startup aesthetics
- redesign the structure completely
- increase corporate feeling
- make typography overly bold
- remove emotional softness

Preserve:
- warmth
- humanity
- Kyoto atmosphere
- emotional pacing
- calmness
- personal tone
- cultural intimacy

Refinement only.
Not reinvention.
